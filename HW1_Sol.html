  2. Mention the appropriate type of sample/data you can get (numerical or categorical), discrete or continuous (if numerical), and size of the sample.

    • An engineer marks each manufactured part in a batch of fifty parts as defective or not. Categorical, -, 50 (It is not incorrect to think of it directly as 0 and 1)
    • You count the number of car arrivals everyday (for a semester) at the COE. Numerical, Discrete, 120
    • We measure heights of people in EGN3443. Numerical, Continuous, 37 (or whatever the number of assumed people in class)
    • You measure waiting times (assume entry to exit) at the COE cafe for people in EGN3443. Numerical, Continuous, 37 (or whatever the number of assumed people in class)
    • We measure temperature at Tallahassee everyday in January. Numerical, Continuous, 31 (days)
    • You roll a die ten times. Numerical, Discrete, 10 (perhaps, it is not incorrect to imagine die like this will result in a non-number)
